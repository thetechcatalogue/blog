## More Agents Is Not Automatically Better

Splitting a workflow across multiple agents adds coordination cost. It helps only when specialization is useful.

## Common Multi-Agent Shapes

Teams often use supervisor models, specialist peers, or pipeline handoffs.

## Handoffs Need Structure

Each handoff should carry goals, state, evidence, and constraints. Without that, agents lose context and repeat work.

## Failure Modes Multiply Quickly

Multi-agent systems can drift, duplicate steps, contradict each other, or hide ownership.

## When The Pattern Is Worth It

Use multi-agent handoffs when one specialist can do part of the job better than a generalist loop.

## Summary

Multi-agent design works when responsibilities are clear, handoffs are structured, and the coordination cost is justified.
