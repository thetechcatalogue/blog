## What Makes an Agent Autonomous

An autonomous agent is given a goal and left to pursue it without step-by-step human guidance. It decides which tools to use, when to replan, how to handle failures, and when the goal has been achieved — all on its own.

## The Observe-Reason-Decide-Act Cycle

Every iteration the agent observes the current state of the environment, reasons about what it implies relative to the goal, decides on the best next action, and executes it. The cycle repeats until a termination condition is met or a resource limit is reached.

## Self-Reflection and Adaptation

Strong autonomous agents include a reflection step that evaluates whether the last action moved them closer to the goal. If not, they adjust their plan, change strategy, or surface the problem. This metacognition is what separates fragile automation from robust agency.

## Resource Management and Safety

Without human oversight, autonomous agents must manage their own resource consumption — API calls, compute, cost, and time. They also need sandboxing, capability restrictions, and clear escalation rules to prevent runaway execution or unintended side effects.

## The Frontier of Agentic AI

Fully autonomous agents represent the current frontier of AI deployment. They enable long-running research, software development, and business automation pipelines that would be impractical to supervise manually. Getting the safety and reliability properties right is the central challenge of agentic AI in 2026.
