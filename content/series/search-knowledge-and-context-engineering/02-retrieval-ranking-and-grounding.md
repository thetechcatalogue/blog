## Retrieval Finds Possibilities

The first retrieval step is usually about recall. It tries to gather enough candidate material that the right answer is somewhere in the set.

## Ranking Chooses What Deserves Attention

Ranking decides which chunks are actually important enough to survive into the final prompt.

## Grounding Depends On Evidence Quality

If the top-ranked material is weak, the answer may still sound fluent while drifting away from the source facts.

## Good Systems Optimize The Whole Path

Search quality is not just about finding documents. It is about finding the right evidence and preserving it through prompt assembly.

## Summary

Retrieval expands the search space, ranking narrows it, and grounding depends on the evidence that remains.
