Retrieval-Augmented Generation, or RAG, combines the power of large language models with external knowledge sources to produce more accurate and grounded responses.

The evaluation process starts by defining clear metrics. Common metrics include factual accuracy, relevance, completeness, and faithfulness to the retrieved context. Automated tools can score responses using these metrics, but human review is often needed for nuanced cases.

Next, we use a test set of queries and expected answers. The RAG system retrieves relevant documents, generates a response, and then each response is scored against the metrics. This helps identify strengths and weaknesses in the retrieval and generation steps.

Quality scoring is not just about numbers. It’s important to analyze failure cases—where did the system hallucinate, miss key facts, or overfit to irrelevant context? These insights guide improvements in retrieval strategies, prompt engineering, and model fine-tuning.

Finally, continuous evaluation is key. As new data and user queries emerge, regularly update your test sets and scoring criteria to ensure the RAG system remains robust and trustworthy.

In summary, RAG evaluation and quality scoring combine automated metrics, human judgment, and iterative improvement to deliver reliable, high-quality AI outputs.
